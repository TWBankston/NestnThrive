What makes a great Home Office?

It’s tempting to start with aesthetics, but a home office must be functional first. The best spaces prioritize ergonomics and workflow before decoration. If your chair hurts your back, the art on the wall doesn't matter.

We recommend starting with your "Interaction Points"—the chair you sit in, the desk you touch, and the monitor you stare at. Invest there first. Everything else is secondary.
